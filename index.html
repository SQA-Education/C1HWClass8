Thriving Under Pressure • Stress Strategies and Modals of Advice

This C1 homework consolidates Class 8 only: stress strategies, modal verbs of advice, and practical recommendations for high-pressure situations.

1. Reading

Advice for Handling Pressure

A stress-management specialist recommends three core strategies for dealing with pressure. First, break large tasks into manageable parts so that the situation feels less overwhelming.

Second, write a worry list. Putting concerns on paper can help people identify which problems they can actually solve and which ones they simply need to manage calmly.

Third, take regular breaks. The expert also argues that people should ask for help when needed and should set realistic expectations.

Match the key term to its best meaning.

Real-life English tip: Advanced advice sounds more persuasive when the modal matches the situation. Choosing between should, ought to, and had better changes the tone.

3. Writing
